About the Flood Disaster

Flood disaster: Flood disasterFlooding is the damage caused by river floods, heavy rains, etc., which generates the land to be submerged by inundation.

Causes

Rainfall (Rainstorm, Typhoon, etc.)

Water that does not penetrate into the ground may create a stream on the surface of the land, such as when there is heavy precipitation.

Storm (Typhoons, etc.)

Storm and winds blowing can obstruct the river flow in the downstream from the sea to the estuary, and in severe cases, seawater can flow back into the river. In such cases, floods can occur without inflow into the river. However, in reality, such a phenomenon is often accompanied by heavy rain of a typhoon.

Snow Melting

In snowy areas, floods caused by snowmelt occur due to abnormal temperature rises in winter, warm strong winds, rainfall, and temperature rises in spring due to seasonal fluctuations. In many cases, the scale of floods is larger due to snowmelt in spring than in rainfall in summer.

Example of Flood

Kinugawa, Ibaraki Prefecture

　

Kinugawa River, Kinugawa is a river on the main island of Honshu in Japan. It flows from the north to the south on the Kantō plain, merging with Tone River. The length of the channel is 176.7km, it is the longest tributary of the Tone River. In the heavy rains in the Kanto and Tohoku regions in 2015, the embankment of the Kinugawa broked, causing large-scale inundation damage over approximately 40km2 of the flat land between the Kinugawa and Kokaigawa rivers. According to the damage summary by the Fire and Disaster Management Agency, 20 people have been confirmed dead. In addition, 82 people were injured, 81 houses were destroyed, 7,090 were partially destroyed, and 1,722 were damaged by non-residential houses.
